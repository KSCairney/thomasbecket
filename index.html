Thomas à Becket

Thomas Becket, Thomas of London, Saint Thomas of Canterbury

Friend of Henry II, Priest, Archbishop of Canterbury, Enemy of Henry II, Martyr, Saint
Henry II meeting with Thomas à Becket
  • Born sometime between 1119 and 1120
  • Worked in the household of Theobald of Bec, the Archbishop of Canterbury
  • Was made Archdeacon of Canterbury in 1154
  • In 1155, Henry II made him Lord Chancellor
  • Fostered Henry II's son Henry, the Young King
  • Made Archbishop of Canterbury in 1162, after the death of Theobald of Bec
  • Henry II hoped that Becket would put the needs of England above the church
  • He did not
  • Becket resigned his Chancellorship, straining his relationship with Henry II
  • Henry pushed for the Constitutions of Clarendon, limiting priestly connections to Rome, in favor of England
  • Becket refused and was forced into exile, a fugitive
  • After reaching a compromise, Becket returned to England
  • Three of the King's subjects attempted to crown Henry the Young King in York
  • Coronation was the right of the Archbishop of Canterbury, Becket excommunicated them
  • Upset with the strife caused by Becket, Henry supposedly muttered "Will no one rid me of this turbulent priest?"
  • Four knights took this as decree, and sought out Becket
  • They slew Becket in Canterbury Cathedral
  • The spot where Thomas Becket was murdered became a shrine, bringing pilgrims from all over Europe
  • Chaucer's The Canterbury Tales is about a band of pilgrims on the road travelling from London to the shrine of Thomas à Becket
  • A shrine is still located at the spot of his martyrdom
